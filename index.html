University of Delhi South Campus

The Institute of Informatics and Communication, University of Delhi, has evolved into one of the premiere institutions of the country since 1997. It targets at fulfilling the increasing demand of trained professionals in the Industry. It has been designed by leading academics in the world of computing as well as communication. Today, the most recognized need in the world of industry and corporate environment is for an informatics professional who not only understands computing and telecommunication technology but also the software related application. The institute, in its nurturing environment gives birth to and nourishes the mind that go on to innovate in the field of communications and information technology.




Mission and Purpose

To contribute to the society through excellence in technical and scientific education and research and to serve as a valuable resource for society and industry.

To take up resource activities for computer education, Skill program Training. Vocational Education Training and training in the education field, Electronics and Information Technology, keeping pace with the modern advancements on one hand and keeping in view the Indian context on the other.

To undertake synergist projects which offer long-term and innovative opportunities for both research and academics.

To develop human potential to its fullest extent so that intellectually capable and imaginatively gifted leaders can emerge in a range of professions.

To establish and maintain labs which are fully equipped with latest gadgets manned by bright minds, the students themselves.

To have tie-ups with other valuable organizations in its area of operations.

CPS Distinguished Lecture Series

IIC, University of Delhi, has a vibrant research environment of the highest standards in Cyber-Physical Systems, AI, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Systems (distributed systems, Networking, Security and Privacy, Cryptography), Data Science, and other related disciplines. CPS covers a multitude of smart devices, ranging from smartphones and tablets to devices and applications which exist in industrial, transportation, medical, home‑security, building automation, emergency management, power, and many other systems. Seminars and workshops organized by IIC are interactive events designed to provide attendees with a technical background in select Cyber-Physical System topics. Professors, experts from Industry, and established practitioners work closely with attendees in tutorials and applications, building meaningful collaborative relationships. The seminar incites faculty members, ongoing researchers, and students to equip and advance their knowledge in Cyber-Physical systems and allied areas and outlines the prospective areas for novel research.
